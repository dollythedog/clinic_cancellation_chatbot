üìã Project Overview

The Problem

  • Last-minute cancellations leave appointment slots unfilled
  • Manual phone calls to waitlist patients are time-consuming
  • Staff burden increases during high cancellation periods
  • Patient access suffers when slots go unfilled
  • Revenue impact from unused appointment capacity

The Solution

  • Intelligent prioritization based on urgency, appointment proximity, and seniority
  • Batch messaging with race-safe confirmation (3 patients at a time)
  • 7-minute hold timers for patient response
  • HIPAA-compliant messaging with minimal PHI exposure
  • Real-time dashboard for staff monitoring and management

‚ú® Key Features

Priority Scoring Algorithm

4-Component Calculation (Higher Score = Higher Priority):

  • Urgent Flag: +30 points (admin-set for critical patients)
  • Manual Boost: 0-40 points (staff override for special cases)
  • Appointment Proximity: 0-20 points (closer current appointment = higher priority)
  • Waitlist Seniority: 0-10 points (time on waitlist)
Example: Patient with urgent flag (30) + 6-month wait (15) + 90 days on waitlist (3) = 48 points

Batch Offering & Hold Timers

How It Works:

  1. Cancellation logged (manual or future EHR integration)
  2. Top 3 candidates selected from waitlist by priority score
  3. Batch SMS sent with 7-minute hold timer
  4. First "YES" response wins the slot (race-safe locking)
  5. Winner confirmed, others notified slot is taken
  6. If no response, next batch sent after timer expires
Result: Fair, transparent, and automated slot allocation

Sample SMS Messages

Initial Offer:

TPCCC: An earlier appointment opened tomorrow at 10:00 AM at Main Clinic. Reply YES to claim or NO to skip. This offer expires in 7 min.

Confirmation (Winner):

TPCCC: Confirmed. You're scheduled Nov 1 at 10:00 AM at Main Clinic. Reply STOP to opt out of future messages.

Slot Taken (Others):

TPCCC: The Nov 1 10:00 AM appointment has been filled. You remain on the waitlist.

‚ö†Ô∏è Risks & Mitigation

Identified Risks

Risk 1: HIPAA Compliance Violation

Mitigation: Pre-production security audit, Twilio BAA, minimal PHI in messages, comprehensive audit logging

Risk 2: Patient Opt-Out Rate Too High

Mitigation: Clear consent process, STOP keyword compliance, respectful contact hours (8am-8pm)

Risk 3: Low Response Rate

Mitigation: A/B test message wording, optimize hold timer duration, analyze response patterns

Risk 4: System Downtime

Mitigation: Fallback to manual process, monitoring alerts, database backups, redundant infrastructure

Contingency Plans

  • System Failure: Dashboard shows failover to manual mode; staff notified via email/Slack
  • Twilio Outage: Graceful degradation; offers queued and sent when service restored
  • Database Issues: Daily backups; 15-minute recovery time objective
  • Message Delivery Failures: Automatic retry logic; staff alerted after 3 failures
  • Patient Complaints: Documented escalation process; immediate opt-out capability
Key Principle: System enhances manual process, doesn't replace it entirely
